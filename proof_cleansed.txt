§ INTRODUCTION AND MATHEMATICAL PRELIMINARIES


Let ℋ be a separable infinite-dimensional Hilbert space equipped with the inner product ⟨·, ·⟩ and the induced norm ·_ℓ^2. We model the core balancing validator as a compact self-adjoint operator T: ℋ→ℋ. 

By the classical Spectral Theorem for compact self-adjoint operators, T admits a unique spectral decomposition corresponding to a countable sequence of real eigenvalues converging to zero, and an associated family of mutually orthogonal projection operators.



§ INFINITE-DIMENSIONAL SPECTRAL FILTERING AND TRUNCATION BOUND


We define the operator T explicitly via its discrete spectrum where the eigenvalues decay quadratically to guarantee strict compactness:

    T = ∫_σ(T)λ  dE(λ) = ∑_n=1^∞λ_n (e_n ⊗ e_n)

where λ_n = 1/n for all n ∈ℤ^+, ensuring that lim_n→∞λ_n = 0, and {e_n}_n=1^∞ forms a complete orthonormal basis of ℋ.

For any high-dimensional language telemetry output mapped onto a normalized unit state vector α = ∑_n=1^∞α_n e_n ∈ℋ satisfying α_ℓ^2 = 1, the continuous verification score evaluates the operator norm boundary. To execute this infinite-dimensional formulation inside finite digital environments (such as Android Termux), we define a finite truncation operator T_N capped at a deterministic dimension cutoff N:

§ RESOLUTION OF THE TRUNCATION LOGICAL GAP


The mathematical gap introduced by discarding the infinite tail components ℰ_tail(N) is strictly upper-bounded by leveraging the integral test for convergence. Since the input coefficients are bounded by the unit sphere configuration (|α_n|≤ 1), the remaining spectral energy satisfies:
