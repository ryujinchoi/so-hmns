\section{Infinite-Dimensional Spectral Decomposition}

By the Spectral Theorem, the operator $T$ decomposes uniquely into an ordered discrete spectrum:
\begin{equation}
T\alpha = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda_n \langle \alpha, e_n \rangle e_n
\end{equation}
where $\{e_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$ constitutes an absolute complete orthonormal basis of $\mathcal{H}$ satisfying $\langle e_i, e_j \rangle = \delta_{ij}$, and the discrete spectrum consists of strictly positive, monotonically decreasing real eigenvalues $\lambda_n = \frac{1}{n}$ for all $n \in \mathbb{Z}^+$. The limit condition $\lim_{n \to \infty} \lambda_n = 0$ strictly guarantees the compactness of $T$.
