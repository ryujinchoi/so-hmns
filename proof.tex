\section{Infinite-Dimensional Spectral Decomposition}

Let $\mathcal{H}$ be a separable infinite-dimensional Hilbert space equipped with the inner product $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$. We model the validation orchestration core as a compact self-adjoint operator $T: \mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{H}$. By the Spectral Theorem, the operator $T$ maps any normalized state vector $\alpha \in \mathcal{H}$ uniquely via:
\begin{equation}
T\alpha = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda_n \langle \alpha, e_n \rangle e_n
\end{equation}
where $\{e_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$ denotes a complete orthonormal basis of $\mathcal{H}$ satisfying $\langle e_i, e_j \rangle = \delta_{ij}$, and the discrete spectrum consists of strictly positive real eigenvalues defined as $\lambda_n = \frac{1}{n}$ for all $n \in \mathbb{Z}^+$. The asymptotic profile $\lim_{n \to \infty} \lambda_n = 0$ strictly guarantees the compactness of $T$.

\section{Rigorous Truncation and Parseval Bound}

In practical computation execution on edge computing infrastructures (e.g., Android Termux), calculation is limited to a finite dimensional cutoff $N$. For any state vector $\alpha = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \alpha_n e_n$ governed by Parseval's identity $\|\alpha\|_{\ell^2}^2 = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \vert\alpha_n\vert^2 = 1$, the continuous energy equation is partitioned as:
\begin{equation}
\|T\alpha\|_{\ell^2}^2 = \sum_{n=1}^{N} \vert\lambda_n \alpha_n\vert^2 + \mathcal{E}_{\text{tail}}(N)
\end{equation}

The residual energy error $\mathcal{E}_{\text{tail}}(N)$ represents the discarded high-frequency components of the spectrum. Because $\vert\alpha_n\vert \le 1$ holds natively on the unit sphere, the tail error is rigorously upper-bounded by the integral convergence criterion of the operator's eigenvalues without injecting artificial data deformation:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{E}_{\text{tail}}(N) = \sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \vert\lambda_n \alpha_n\vert^2 \le \sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \lambda_n^2 = \sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} < \int_{N}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx = \frac{1}{N}
\end{equation}

Consequently, accounting for hardware floating-point constraints $\epsilon_{\text{mach}}$, the physical system preserves the structural topology with a truncation error strictly bounded by $\mathcal{O}(N^{-1}) + \mathcal{O}(N \cdot \epsilon_{\text{mach}})$, closing all analytical logical gaps for edge orchestration frameworks.
