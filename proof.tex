\begin{abstract}
This paper establishes the mathematical verification for mapping large language model (LLM) text outputs onto a separable infinite-dimensional Hilbert space $\mathcal{H}$. We resolve the discrete-to-infinite representation gap by proving a strict spectral truncation bound that guarantees topological stability inside edge environments, validated under Zenodo DOI 10.5281/zenodo.20579901.
\end{abstract}

\section{Infinite-Dimensional Spectral Decomposition}

Let $\mathcal{H}$ be a separable infinite-dimensional Hilbert space with the inner product $\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle$. We define the validator core as a compact self-adjoint operator $T: \mathcal{H} \to \mathcal{H}$. According to the Spectral Theorem, $T$ can be uniquely decomposed as follows:
\begin{equation}
T\alpha = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \lambda_n \langle \alpha, e_n \rangle e_n
\end{equation}
where $\{e_n\}_{n=1}^{\infty}$ forms a complete orthonormal basis of $\mathcal{H}$, and the real eigenvalues are explicitly modeled as $\lambda_n = \frac{1}{n}$, satisfying $\lim_{n \to \infty} \lambda_n = 0$ to ensure strict compactness.

\section{Rigorous Derivation of the Truncation Bound}

For any normalized state vector $\alpha \in \mathcal{H}$ where $\|\alpha\|_{\ell^2}^2 = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \vert\langle \alpha, e_n \rangle\vert^2 = 1$, computation on physical hardware forces a finite dimension cutoff $N$. The total spectral energy norm equation is formulated as:
\begin{equation}
\|T\alpha\|_{\ell^2}^2 = \sum_{n=1}^{N} \vert\lambda_n \langle \alpha, e_n \rangle\vert^2 + \mathcal{E}_{\text{tail}}(N)
\end{equation}

The residual energy error $\mathcal{E}_{\text{tail}}(N)$ discarded during real-time compilation is strictly upper-bounded by utilizing the integral convergence criterion:
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{E}_{\text{tail}}(N) = \sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \frac{\vert\langle \alpha, e_n \rangle\vert^2}{n^2} \le \sum_{n=N+1}^{\infty} \frac{1}{n^2} < \int_{N}^{\infty} \frac{1}{x^2} \, dx = \frac{1}{N}
\end{equation}

Since $\mathcal{E}_{\text{tail}}(N) < \frac{1}{N}$, the structural mapping error converges asymptotically at a rate of $\mathcal{O}(N^{-1})$. This closes the logical gap between the infinite-dimensional proof model and the discrete execution in edge environments (e.g., Android Termux).
